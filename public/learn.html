How to Get Started — Beginner Mode

Goal: Hold a tokenised U.S. Treasury product in self-custody and earn yield passively. Follow the steps exactly. No prior crypto experience required.

1
Set up your hardware wallet (Ledger)

Why: Self-custody means you control the keys; exchanges can freeze funds or fail.

  1. Download Ledger Live from the official site. Install and open it.
  2. Initialize the Ledger device → create a new wallet → write the 24-word recovery phrase on paper (never digital).
  3. Update device firmware in Ledger Live.
  4. In My Ledger, install the Ethereum app on the device.
  5. (Optional) Connect Ledger to MetaMask so you can sign transactions with hardware security.

Checklist: 24-word phrase saved offline • PIN set • Ethereum app installed.

Safety: Keep ~$10–$30 worth of ETH for gas fees. Send it to your Ledger address first.

2
Acquire USDC to fund the purchase

Why: Most tokenised Treasuries mint/redeem using USDC on Ethereum.

  1. On a reputable exchange (Coinbase, Kraken), buy USDC (Ethereum network).
  2. Withdraw USDC to your Ledger-controlled Ethereum address. Paste the address exactly; send a small test first.
  3. Confirm USDC arrival in your wallet. If not visible, add the USDC contract manually.

Gotcha: Ensure you selected ERC-20 (Ethereum) network. Wrong network = permanent loss.

3
Create issuer account & complete KYC (e.g., Ondo USDY)

Why: These hold real T-bills; issuers must verify investor identity and whitelist your wallet.

  1. Create an account on the issuer's site (e.g., Ondo Finance).
  2. Complete identity verification (government ID + liveness check).
  3. Submit your exact Ledger wallet address to be whitelisted.
  4. Wait for "approved/whitelisted" status (usually quick during business hours).

Note: Availability depends on your jurisdiction. U.S. persons may face restrictions.

4
Mint the Treasury token (e.g., USDY)
  1. Connect your wallet via MetaMask (linked to Ledger). Select Ethereum mainnet.
  2. On the issuer's "Mint" page, pick the token (USDY), amount ($1,000+), and set source to USDC.
  3. First transaction: Approve USDC spend (one-time). Sign with Ledger.
  4. Second transaction: Mint the token. Sign with Ledger.
  5. Wait for confirmation; the token will appear in your wallet.

Yield: No staking needed. Returns reflect in the token's redemption value over time; you just hold.

5
Redeem back to USDC (exit anytime)
  1. On the issuer's "Redeem" page, select your token balance (e.g., USDY) and target USDC.
  2. Confirm the whitelisted wallet will receive USDC (must match KYC address).
  3. Sign the redemption transaction(s) with Ledger.
  4. When USDC arrives, withdraw to your exchange → sell to fiat → transfer to your bank.

Timing: On-chain is instant; fiat depends on your bank. No lock-ups under normal conditions.

!
Safety, Costs & Common Gotchas
  • Network correctness: Always use Ethereum (ERC-20) for USDC unless the issuer explicitly supports another chain.
  • Gas budget: Keep a small ETH buffer for approvals, mint, and redeem transactions.
  • Whitelisting: Redemptions require the same whitelisted address used at KYC time.
  • Impostor contracts: Add token contracts only from the issuer's official UI/docs, never third-party.
  • Tax: AU/NZ treatment varies. Keep timestamps, transaction hashes, and issuer statements.
  • Off-ramp: Your exchange must support your bank region (AUD/NZD) for smooth cash-out.

Beginner FAQ
  • Do I need to stake USDY? No. Just holding reflects the yield via the issuer's mechanism.
  • Can I lose funds? Smart-contract, issuer, and custody risks exist. Use official UIs and hardware wallets.
  • US person? Check issuer eligibility rules; additional restrictions may apply.
  • Which chain? Use the chain the issuer supports (usually Ethereum mainnet for USDY).
  • How long for KYC approval? Usually hours to 1 business day. Faster during weekday business hours.
